Beijing-Shandong cuisine

Shandong Cuisine

Shandong cuisine is the first of the four major cuisines in China. Its cooking techniques and tools are different from other countries' traditional cooking techniques, laying the foundation of Chinese cuisine and representing traditional Chinese cuisine.

The outstanding cooking method of Shandong cuisine is stir frying. Stir frying is a cooking method that is heated in a very short time on a large fire.

With widely distributed mountains, rivers and plains. Shandong produces a variety of food ingredients. Shandong Province has the longest coastline in China. Shandong cuisine chefs are excellent at cooking seafood.

Beijing Cuisine

The most representative of Beijing cuisine is the palace food of the Ming and Qing dynasties. It is generally believed that the imperial diet in the Qing Dynasty was based on Shandong cuisine, Suzhou and Hangzhou catering and the inherent diet of the Manchu people. The palace banquets represented by the Manchu and Han banquets shows the luxurious characteristics of Beijing cuisine.
